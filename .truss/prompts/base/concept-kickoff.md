You are entering the **concept** phase. Discovery and validation are behind you;
the job now is synthesis, not new exploration. Build the strongest honest case for
what to do with this idea — and be willing to conclude "park it".

Before writing, load `VISION.md`, `market.md`, `customer.md`, the assumption log,
and `state/decisions.md`. Then draft `concept.md`:

1. **Concept summary** — the idea in plain language: for whom, what problem, what
   solution, why it beats the alternative. No pitch gloss.
2. **Value proposition** — what concretely changes for the target customer, and
   why they would switch from the status quo.
3. **Key assumptions and status** — the load-bearing beliefs, each marked
   validated / partly / untested with a pointer to the evidence. Riskiest first.
4. **Risks and open questions** — what could still sink this; separate risks you
   would accept from ones that must be resolved before any commitment.
5. **Recommendation** — pursue or park, and why. If pursue, the smallest next step
   that buys the most certainty; if park, what would have to change to reopen it.

Stay tied to evidence gathered earlier. Where the case rests on an untested
assumption, say so rather than papering over it. The phase exits when `concept.md`
has a real `#Recommendation` and the human records a pursue/park decision as a
D-entry (`doctor --gate` checks this).
